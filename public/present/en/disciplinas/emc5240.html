Introduction to Machining Processes - EMC5240

SUMMARY

PROGRAM

  1. Introduction and presentation of the discipline program; definition of the main machining processes.
  2. Dimensional precision on fabrication, precision requirement, geometrical errors on fabrication, roughness, measurement principles.
  3. Machining fundaments – definitions of moves, directions and cutting speeds from the machining processes, definitions of the main process features and machining parameters, as well as the geometry of cutting tool, basement for machining with defined geometry tool – the cutting edge, the cutting process, strength on cutting edge, cutting tool wear.
  4. Materials for non-defined and defined geometry cutting tools, historical retrospective of materials applied on cutting tools (metallic materials, carbides, ceramic materials, hard non-metallic materials). Tools set up, tools standardization, initial settings and cares with cutting tools.
  5. Lubricant coolant fluids and their functions, types, applying, influences on machining processes and proper choice.
  6. Machinablility and its definition and criterions (tool life, machining forces, surface quality, chip shape); influent causes on machinability.
  7. Machining conditions and their economical determinations, optimization and technological limits.
  8. Machine-tools: history, definitions, systems and sub-systems, components, requirement, building and selection.
  9. Lathes: turning process and tools, lathes types, sub-systems, applications.
  10. Drilling machines: drilling process, reaming and threading, drills, reamers, screw taps, types of drilling machines, sub-systems, applications.
  11. Milling machines: milling process, milling cutters, milling machines types, sub-systems, applications
  12. Gear generators and gear calculation.
  13. Boring machines and planners: boring and planning processes, boring and planning tools, boring machines types, sub-systems, applications.
  14. Broaching machines and broaches.
  15. Grinding machines, grinding process and grinding wheel.
  16. Lapping, burnishing, polishing, machining with abrasive paper, blasting.
  17. Computerized systems for machining (CNC etc).
  18. Non-conventional machining processes.
  19. Machining special subjects.
  20. Exercise classes, laboratories, exams.
