Metal-mechanical Fabrication Processes - EMC5245

SUMMARY

Ample concept of metallic materials and fabrication processes on metal-mechanic sector. Heat treatment. Fabrication process with and without material removal. Processes: machining, forming, foundry, welding. Slight knowledge of special fabrication processes (electroerosion, electrochemical erosion, ultrasound, electronic beam among others). Description of various equipments in order to automatize fabrication processes. Slight knowledge of interconnection with other sectors (project, planning and assembling etc).

PROGRAM

  1. Crystalline structure of metallic materials. Phases diagram. Heat treatment, TTT and CCT curves, alloy elements effect.
  2. Hot, warm and cold forming: generalities and processes. Forging, lamination, drawing, extrusion and plates working: generalities, application economical aspects, equipments and tools.
  3. Precision foundry processes. Foundry fundaments and importance. Slight knowledge of solidification and melted parts project. “Shell-molding” process. Investment process. Foundry under pressure.
  4. Introduction to fabrication processes with chip removal. Fundaments of defined geometry tool machining. Defined geometry tool. Cutting fluid. Machinability. Machining economical aspects. Fundaments of non-defined geometry tool machining. Non-defined geometry tool. Non-conventional machining processes.
  5. Welding. Energy source for arc welding: principles and static characteristics. Alternating current sources. Continuous current sources. Special sources. Study of common characteristics of arc processes with consumable electrodes. Welding techniques. Definitions. Study of metallic transfer. Gas effect. TIG process: equipments and operation principles, applications, materials, alternating current welding, beaten current welding. The plasma and microplasma processes: equipments and operation principles, applications, materials, alternating current welding, beaten current welding. Electronic beam welding: process fundaments, welding characteristics, applications. Precision union by brazing. Brazing fundaments. Manual and automatic brazing with flow. High temperature brazing (without flow) with special union technique.
