Machining Technology with Defined Geometry Cutting Tools - EMC5275

SUMMARY

Machining processes technology which apply defined geometry cutting tools. Empirical study of chip formation mechanisms and wear mechanisms. Presentation of cutting tools materials. Study of cutting fluid influence on machining processes. Study of machinability criterions and machinability analysis of different workpiece materials. Economical determination of machining conditions. Machining operations optimization.

PROGRAM

  1. Discipline program introduction and presentation (2h)
  2. Dimensional precision on fabrication, precision requirements, fabrication geometric errors, roughness, metrology aspects revision, fabrication applicable instruments (1h)
  3. Basement for machining with defined geometry tools – cutting edge, cutting process, stresses on cutting edge, tool wear (6h)
  4. Cutting tools materials and defined geometry cutting tools, historical retrospective of materials applied as cutting tools (metallic materials, carbides, ceramic materials, non-metallic hard materials), tools configurations, cutting tools standardization, cutting tools preparation (12h)
  5. Lubricant fluids, cutting fluids utilities, cutting fluids types, adjusted cutting fluid application, cutting fluid influence on machining process, cutting fluids selection (3h)
  6. Machinability, machinability definition, machinability criterions (tool life, machining forces, surface quality, chips shape), influents features on machinability, ferrous machining as a function of carbon text, influence of other alloy elements on ferrous materials machinability, ferrous materials machinability as function of heat treatment, examples of many ferrous materials machinability, machinability, copper alloys machinability, nickel alloys machinability, cobalt alloys machinability, titanium alloys machinability (12h)
  7. Economical determination of machining conditions, machining conditions optimization, technological limits of cutting conditions, monitoring system and control system of machining processes (3h)
  8. Practical applications and case study of machining conditions optimization and machine-tools choice (3h)
  9. Practical class about machining (3h)
  10. Exams (1, 2 and recuperation) (9h)
