Flow Drilling and Form Threading - FURCON

DESCRIPTION

The studies on Flow Drilling and Form Threading began on 1991, on a Master Work that studied the use of automatic feeds on flow drilling and form threading processes. Many ferrous and non-ferrous materials were analyzed, focusing on flowed bushing burr quality and produced threads resistance.

From that on, the group made many researches with industries such as Mercedes Benz of Brazil, Yanes Minas Limited, Reubli Inc. There were also a cooperation accord with Flowdrill B.V. of Holland and research projects integrated with UBA – Buenos Aires (Argentina), Tarapacá University – Arica (Chile), Institut für Produtkionstechnik und Logistik – IPL (Germany).

Besides those works, another Master Work was concluded. On this one, a comparative analysis between the use of constant work conditions on process with viable work conditions was made, basing on process variables and work results. It was developed also a metallurgical study of the process, aiming on determining the microstructural transformations, emphasizing recrystallization and grain refinement phenomenon.

Nowadays the group develops one Master and two Doctor Works. The Master Work studies the application of ceramic materials for flow drill tools creation. Therefore, the necessary technological alterations, the developed characteristics (generated efforts and temperature), and the application results must be determinate.

One of the Doctor Works aims to optimize the flow drilling process through tool geometry influence study on material movement, as well as on generated efforts and temperatures during process execution. The other Doctor Work aims to understand the relation between process conditions execution and their influence on microstructural alterations and on mechanical properties alterations of the materials, which is on the case the hot-rolled low-carbon steel.
