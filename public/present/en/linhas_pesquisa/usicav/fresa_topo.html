CAVITIES FABRICATION
BY END MILLING

OBJECTIVES

This research area objective is to study 5 axes HSC (High Speed Cutting) end milling processes for mold industries.

The 5 axes HSC milling represents, for the modern industry, a rationalization way to the process. With 5 axes milling, it is possible to machine complex surfaces and also to have a better crest control and a better process rigidity, since the tool reaches deeper regions with smaller shank lengths.

Very high cutting speeds generate an increase on rate removal, and the specific machining forces become incredibly smaller. Therefore, the process stresses are reduced, making possible to avoid thermals and chemicals modifies that might change the material characteristics, or cause problems on further operations.

With 5 axes HSC milling, a technological alteration of the machining process sequence occurs because it is possible to reduce or even eliminate further operations (electrical discharge machining and some polishing operations, for example).

From the cutting characterization on tool tip, to machine-tool development, because of the system structural and dynamical characteristics and specific software and hardware development, regarding the machine complex movements, the technological development is continuous

PROCEDURES

For the process recital, the general machining technological aspects are studied. It is also defined specific cutting strategies for cavities milling, aiming at surface finishing optimization.
All assays are made based on experimental planning, and analyzed through statistics data treatment seeking the perfect comprehension of the results.
A portion of the experiments is developed in cooperation with Fraunhofer Institut für Produktionstechnologie, in Aachen, Germany, inside the same assays systematics.

RESULTS

After obtaining the basic information for industry needs, the technological diffusion is essential for the research continuity. It is intended to create basis for the industrial use of this research in Brazil.
This work represents for the Mechanical Engineering Department/UFSC one of the technological basis on formation of an Excellency Centre on injection molds area.
