Cavity Machining - USICAV

DESCRIPTION

The USICAV group – Cavity Machining – focuses on defined geometry tools machining used on cavity fabrication. The USICAV objectives are milling and boring study and optimization. Through the measurement of surface quality related parameters and shape precision, as well as process related parameters – tool dynamic characteristics and cutting forces during machining – the USICAV attempts to create conditions for tool characterizing and for process optimization aiming the transfer or application of this knowledge in industry.

DEVELOPMENT AREAS

  • FORCES THEORETICAL-EXPERIMENTAL DETERMINATION AND LOW RIGIDITY WORKPIECES DYNAMIC ANALYSIS BY FINITE ELEMENTS METHOD
  • MILLING PROCESS STABILITY AT HIGH CUTTING SPEED
  • FORCES MODELING AND SIMULATION AND TOOL DEFLECTION IN MILLING
  • ANALYSIS OF END MILLERS DEFLECTION INFLUENCE ON SURFACE QUALITY
  • INDUSTRIAL GRAPHITE HIGH SPEED MILLING
  • BORING WITH ADJUSTABLE RUNOUT HEAD AND BALANCING SYSTEM
  • CAVITY FABRICATION BY END MILLING
  • GROOVE MILLING WITH HSS (HIGH SPEED STEEL) END MILL
  • TECHNOLOGICAL FEATURES OF END MILLING PROCESS WITH MINIMAL CUTTING FLUID QUANTITY
  • BORE-MILLING OF THREADS IN MAGNESIUM
