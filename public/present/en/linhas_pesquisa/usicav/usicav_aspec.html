TECHNOLOGICAL ASPECTS OF END MILLING PROCESS
WITH MINIMAL CUTTING FLUID QUANTITY

OBJECTIVES

The main objective of this work is to obtain technological information in order to compare actual production conditions with minimum fluid quantity machining, by means of forces behavior and tool life analysis.
Beside tools, machines-tools and cutting parameters optimization, another emergent world tendency is the companies concern regarding production technological aspects. Therefore, with the growing pressure from environmental organizations to reduce the industrial production impact on environment, and with the sprouting of more and more severe laws against infractors, the importance to minimize the environment prejudices caused by machining processes becomes evident.

PROCEDURES

On this research there were used high speed steel (HSS) and carbide mills, coated and uncoated, all with same geometry. The assays were made on the way to guarantee the repeatability of machining conditions for all tool groups, varying only the cutting fluid type. On this study it was applied dry machining, ideal from ecological viewpoint, also the minimal cutting fluid quantity machining (mineral and vegetal) and machining with emulsion.

During experiments, the wear evolution on face and flank of every tool edge was monitored. The material used on assays was SAE 1040 steel and the cutting conditions for roughing accorded the manufacturer catalog.

RESULTS

This work has specific characteristics inherent to process, material and tool. However, it is possible to establish some important considerations that contribute to a more generic way with the studied theme.
The use of emulsion is much spread out on the productive mean, however it is observed on assays with this type of fluid that the tool life is reduced due to high refrigeration rate which causes severe thermal shocks on cutting edges.

The employment of minimal cutting fluid quantity brought also good results. On assays with minimization, the tools had very good performance, although on machining with mineral oil as cutting fluid there was a tendency to better results.
