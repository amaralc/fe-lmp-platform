FORCES MODELING AND SIMULATION
AND TOOL DEFLECTION IN MILLING
 

OBJECTIVES

The objective of this work is to model, simulate and validate in computers certain characteristics of the milling process, like cutting forces and tool deflection.

The development of computers with faster processors, the creation of softwares and environment of more sophisticated computational models, and the computational simulation appliance grow constantly in all engineering areas, including machining.

The simulation aims to save time and resources, by studying and changing the process parameters without spending material, machine time, employee time etc. With simulation, it is expected to find more favorable regions for the process and to reduce the number of assays on shop floor. The simulation ought to give lines of direction for assays and point the subjects that should be boarded and modified.

PROCEDURES

The models used on simulations are based on existing models in literature. There are made previous assays in order to calculate the wanted constants for the simulation and also to calibrate the model.

These constants are stored in a database for further study. The deflection may cause excessive vibrations in the process.

The input parameters for simulation are feed per teeth, cutting depth, cutting width, tool rotation, tool diameter and length. The obtained results are cutting forces and tool deflection.

RESULTS

With simulation it is expected to find more suitable regions for materials machining, reducing the number of required assays.
There are made assays in order to validate and improve simulations, thus their results can get the closest as possible of the reality.
