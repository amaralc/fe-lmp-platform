FORCES THEORETICAL-EXPERIMENTAL DETERMINATION AND LOW
RIGIDITY WORKPIECES DYNAMIC ANALYSIS BY FINITE ELEMENTS METHOD

 

OBJECTIVES

The objective of this work is to experimentally-theoretically determinate forces on face milling, in order to use them as input data on dynamic analysis by finite elements on low rigidity workpieces submitted to alternate efforts on milling process.

The forces study on milling is important for the machine-tool parts such as bearings, base, drive, guides, tool and workpiece clamping system, also on cutting parameters settings, wear mechanisms comprehension, vibrations amplitude prevision and an estimative for the obtainable machining precision prevision.

The finite elements method is greatly established as a powerful and popular numerical tool, initially acclaimed on structural analysis applications. At the present time, however, this method is being applied successfully in numerous areas, including machining.

PROCEDURES

The cutting forces generated on turning and milling processes can be reproduced by models proposed by Kienzle and Altintas, among others.

The forces formulation on face milling woks as input data for finite elements in low rigidity workpieces, in which different cutting strategies are evaluated for vibration effects reduction.

RESULTS

With the simulations it is intended to identify the suitable cutting strategies in order to reduce vibrations in low rigidity workpieces, still on project stage, or to allow the cutting processes to be reviewed and corrected before the process start.
