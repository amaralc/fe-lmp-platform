BORE - MILLING OF
THREADS ON MAGNESIUM

OBJECTIVES

The drilling process followed by threading is generally very applied in industry. Aiming specially fabrication costs reducing, the drilling and internal milling process (Bohrgewinde-fräsen) was developed. This process uses an only tool, which executes drilling, reaming and, in sequence, threading.


Drilling and threads internal milling tool

Magnesium has a growing importance in industry, specially due to its low weight (2/3 of aluminum’s weight).
This work’s main objective, collectively with Institut für Produktionstechnik und Logistik from Kassel University – Germany, is to improve this type of tool performance on magnesium dry machining. Among analyzed topics, it can be brought up forces behavior, wear types and evolution mechanisms, and the influence of input parameters (tool geometry, cutting speed, feed, cutting depth, cutting width etc).

PROCEDURES

The magnesium adhesion on the tool during dry machining causes a few problems that compromise the use of this process in threads. A tool premature break usually occurs due to drill flute blockage.
The basic information obtainment process about drilling and threads internal milling tools use, as well as magnesium machinability, begins with a bibliographical review about the theme on national and international specialized publications. This research generates a database with information of the state of the art of this type of process.

Through assays and results analysis it will be possible to identify the tool break causes, providing a direct performance on the problems and, consequently, the process optimization. It becomes viable, thus, this process implement on industrial scale.

RESULTS

The expected results will be useful for the threads fabrication process to become more well-known and to be applied securely and with good productivity with better scientific fundament.

It is expected, also, for this research to be the beginning and the basis for future developments on drilling and threading areas, always aiming ecological machining and new technologies development.
