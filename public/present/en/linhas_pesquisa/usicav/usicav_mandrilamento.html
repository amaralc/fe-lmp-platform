BORING WITH ADJUSTABLE RUNOUT
HEAD AND BALANCING SYSTEM

OBJECTIVES

Although boring tools rarely reach 15% of all machining tools, they are extremely important on process productivity. Since boring is often applied on parts final machining, that have already a high aggregated cost from several hours of previous machining, the rubbish making on this level is highly unwelcome.
The objectives of this work are verification and comparison of dynamical characteristics and influence on machined surface quality, as well on shape and dimensional precision on boring equipped with tool runout and balancing system adjustment.

PROCEDURES

The unbalancing of boring heads caused by cutting tool runout influences on machine vibration level, which influences on machined holes surface quality and shape precision. Aiming to contribute on precision boring process optimization, it is intended to compare the most known boring heads available for sale. All selected boring heads present tool runout adjustment, working on holes from 10 to 25 mm of diameter. Some heads have integrated balancing system. The materials used on assays are aluminum with 15% of silicon, cast iron GGG 30, SAE steel 1045 and SAE steel 4340.

There are 5 interesting characteristics on this work.

  • Dynamics characteristics measurement: with the vibration level measurement, it is intended to evaluate the balancing system influence on machine spindle – head system vibration.
  • Influence on surface quality: evaluation of balancing system influence on surface finishing through machined test bars roughness measurement.
  • Influence on shape precision: evaluation of balancing system influence on bored holes circularity.
  • Regulation precision: for a specific diameter variation it is made an runout regulation and, after boring the hole, the final diameter is measured in order to compare the last one with the pre-meditated diameter.
  • Regulation time: for a specific diameter, the necessary time to reach that measure is measured. Thus, such the head regulation as the necessary number of passes to reach the measure within the desired tolerance are considerated.
