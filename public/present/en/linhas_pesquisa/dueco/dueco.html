Ecological Machining Development - DUECO

DESCRIPTION

In 1996 LMP started this research line with one doctor work of the mechanical engineering post-graduation course, aiming to evaluate the technological influences of minimization of cutting fluids in machining processes with defined geometry cutting tools.
During this period, searching for partners for this research, the great importance of the theme was evident, and also the urgent need to create new alternative ways, seeking to avoid aggressions to the environment by the manufacturing processes.

For this reason, the group got a significant growth on the way to be able to serve conveniently the research interests.
This theme demands a multidisciplinary performance, reason why LMP sustain a partnership with Sanitarian and Ambient Engineering Course – UFSC and Fraunhofer- Institut für Produktionstechnologie – Aachen – BRD.

Nowadays, integrated to this research group, there are one doctor work and three master works, allowing in this way the generation of knowledge needed in this research stage.
In order to support a better performance, the group is divided in two subgroups:

  • Machining Processes Group (GPU) – deals with technological aspects of machining processes
  • Environment Group (GMA) – deals with ecological aspects of machining processes

The leading DUECO’s aim is to produce basic knowledge about cutting fluids minimization and/or elimination. The cutting fluids are confirmed to be responsible for the pollution and aggressive aspects of the machining processes.

Through the development of this knowledge it will be possible to contribute to metal-mechanic companies “ecological evolution” (adaptation of processes to the new ambient laws).
More specifically the objectives are divided like this:


Machining Processes Group: Its objective is to evaluate the real technological influences over the main machining processes with defined geometry cutting tools (turning, drilling and milling) done by the reduction and/or elimination of cutting fluids in this operations. Generally, the evaluation includes:

  • Technological aspects – machining process efficiency
  • Economical aspects – costs involved in processes

Environment Group: Aims to technically support the Precision Engineering Laboratory on the development of ecological machining. The main purpose is to define a method to quantify the ambient impact produced by each machining operation, caused by the use of cutting fluids, allowing this way a evaluate a total impact caused by a determined production line.

  • Technological aspects – impact caused to environment
