CUTTING FLUIDS VOLUME OPTIMIZATION IN MACHINING PROCESSES

 

INTRODUCTION

Cutting Fluids had been used in machining processes since anti¬quities. The significant productivity raised documents, caused by the use of cutting fluids during machining, are related to 100 years ago. In 1883 F.W.Taylor proved that the cutting speed can be raised up to 30 - 40% through the interface work piece-cutting tool flooded with strong water flow. Since then, the cutting fluid development has been growing to achieve better perfor¬mance on machining.

This development concern about economic and technologic aspects has forgotten the ecologic aspects. Three decades ago, motivated by constants problems manifested in machine operators, studies started to evaluate the hazard levels (for men and environment) of cutting fluids used in fabrication processes. Within this study lots of products were forbidden due to their high toxic level, while others less toxic were limited on their concentration.

As cutting fluids total elimination in machining processes is not possible, the optimization of its use on the various processes and the new nontoxic product development seem to be the best alternative to reduce the environment impact caused by its use.

OBJECTIVES

The studies in this area aim to fit the machining processes inside the environment demands that are gradually emerging by the need to reduce and/or eliminate the several sources of aggressions to environment.

Being cutting fluids one of the main fabrication processes pollution agents, its essential to focus the research on this area.

RESEARCH DEVELOPMENT

LMP, predicting the future Brazilian productive processes need to adapt itself to the international production exigencies, started in 1997 to study this area. In the beginning there are evaluated the real cutting fluids influences in the traditional machining processes with defined geometry cutting tools (turning, drilling, milling).
This evaluation is made on:

  • Cutting Tool
    o Life time
    o Wear shapes and types
    o Cutting forces
    o Work temperature
  • Workpiece
    o Dimensional quality
    o Surface quality
    o Cheap shape and type

Through fluid type variations, application method and quantity applied on process, it is possible to establish the technological influences caused by these changes.
With this methodology it is possible to generate basic knowledge allowing the identification of the main benefits caused by cutting fluids in each process. That knowledge, if conveniently interpreted, is able to modify the cutting fluids characteristics reducing or eliminating the environment impact of the several fabrication processes.
