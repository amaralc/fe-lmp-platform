CUTTING FLUIDS MINIMIZATION AND
ELIMINATION ON THE DRILLING PROCESS

 

INTRODUCTION

In order to improve machining operation performance, technologically as well as economically, cutting fluids are often used on different machining processes. This product, however, is proved to be harmful to the machine tool operators’ health and also to environment. In the past years the ambient agencies, the governmental laws, society and international market pressure and request has become more exigent, aiming the environment impact reduction caused by the several production sectors, among them the metal mechanic. As consequence, dry machining and minimal cutting fluids use (MQFC) has been showing as a strong alternative to the fabrication processes.

Drilling with helicoidally tools is one of the most utilized machining operations. New coatings that increase tool wearing resistance and allow high speed machining contribute to these new tools application in dry machining.

The ecological machining development study group works in LMP since 1996, where it has developed researches on this theme. During this period several master works and one doctor work were developed, together with specific studies for partner companies, aiming to develop a good knowledge collection on this theme.

OBJECTIVES

The research maintenance on this area seek to evaluate the technological drilling aspects with fast steel helicoidally tools ( normal, special with TiNAl, TiN coating and coated with TiN + MoS2) and hard metal P40 ( with TiN coating, TiNAl) dry machining, minimal and different cutting fluid flow. This study aims to improve technological, economical and ecological efficiency on drilling process, providing technical support to the actual fabrication processes. It also aims new technologies development to supply the fabrication processes needs, more exigent each time.

RESEARCH DEVELOPMENT

Process base studies had been developed, creating basic knowledge and process parameters dominion. Actually the group is studying new technological alternatives available in the market to improve the operation efficiency, satisfying the ecological exigencies imposed by the ambient agencies. In other words, the studies are directed in a way to reduce the process ambient impact, sustaining and/or improving the actual process efficiency indexes.


In this way, the studies include:

  • tool material
  • coating material
  • tool geometry
  • process parameters
  • machining strengths
  • process cutting fluids
  • chip shapes and types
  • new process conceptions
