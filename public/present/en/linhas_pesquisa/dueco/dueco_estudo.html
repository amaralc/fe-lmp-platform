STUDY OF TECHNOLOGICAL INFLUENCE OF
CUTTING FLUIDS ON TURNING PROCESS

 

INTRODUCTION

In order to improve machining operation performance, technologically as well economically, cutting fluids are often used on different machining processes. The growing ambient agencies pressure to reduce the production impact over the environment and the elaboration of more strict laws, stimulate the development of all or any work concerned to reduce the fabrication processes ambient impact and contribute to achieve the clean production.

As consequence, dry machining and minimal cutting fluids use (MQFC) has been showing as a strong alternative to the fabrication processes. Among all machining processes, turning is one of the most utilized. New coatings that increase tool wearing resistance and allow high temperature machining contribute to these new tools application on dry machining.

The ecological machining development study group work in LMP since 1996, where it has been developing researches on this theme. During this period several master works and one doctor work were developed, together with specific studies to partner companies, aiming to develop a good knowledge collection on this theme.

OBJECTIVES

This work development seek to evaluate the tool wearing manner, machining strengths and superficial quality obtained from turning process with hard metal inserts. In this study five conditions are evaluated concerning cutting fluids: dry machining, minimal cutting fluid use (MQFC), vegetal oil, mineral oil and molybdenum disulfide. The experiments are carried through to longitudinal cylindrical turning.

RESEARCH DEVELOPMENT

Aiming to gain technological information to the productive systems, inserts often used in the shop floor were chosen.

In this tests it is analyzed the tool wearing evolution, machining strength behavior (cutting force, feed force and passive force) and the surface quality obtained with each insert. In the final results it will be able to observe the fluid elimination influence on this process, once cutting fluid, viewed by the ecological aspect, has shown as a harmful agent against the environment. In this context, this work development is perfectly justified.
