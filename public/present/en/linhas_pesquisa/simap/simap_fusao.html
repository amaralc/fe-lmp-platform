SENSORS FUSION THROUGH NEURAL NETS

INTRODUCTION

The need for precision and trustworthy systems in acquisition by sensors of the signals generated in machining is fundamental in cases where the monitorization and process control are present.

When data are collected directly, there is a process stop problem so that measurements can be done. The direct measure, on the other hand, is done on-line, but the obtained data trustworthiness is lost. The solution found was a multisensor system elaboration, where each sensor becomes complementary to the other, increasing in the on-line system the obtained data trustworthiness.

The various signals caught by the multiple sensors, after been duly treated, are then combined through a neural net, whose answer can be used as a control and system reaction variable.

OBJECTIVE

Develop and implement a model based in connection, able to realize different signals fusion caught by multiple sensors, in order to obtain an answer variable, adjusted to a possible decision taking.

RESEARCH DEVELOPMENT

The unique sensor application in the on-line monitorization can show doubtful and incomplete data, beyond that, it can be insensible to a certainly process alteration. Employing multisensors, the sensor information can be weak and scarce, while the other can be safe and robust. The use of strength and acoustic emission sensors has shown to be efficient in the monitorization of metal cutting tool condition, mainly by the strong relation among signals with wearing and harming, beyond the great capacity to self-complement.

A neural net is able to suppress the deficient information of a signal related to a detected problem, incapacitating the entrance of it and overlapping it (through the weight alteration in connections among the net levels) with the entrance of other relevant signals. A generic and flexible model, making use of strength and acoustic emission signals, will be obtained through deductive knowledge of the already existing and available theory, and inductive knowledge, obtained through tests realization with controlled variables.
