Inteligent Systems for Process Automatization and Monitorization - SIMAP

DESCRIPTION

The search for higher quality and productivity indexes in the metal-mechanic industry prioritizes the production time reduction and better machined finishing, providing a natural machine and equipments evolution.

The growing tendency is in the control systems utilization that interacts with machine, automatizing the process and reducing considerably the unproductive times.
Such times are caused mainly due to process interruptions, necessary to make corrections decurrently from unexpected tool changes.

This corrective maintenance is usually accomplished after a process failure, detected by the operator, through finished workpiece observation.
With this, it has become necessary to develop intelligent systems that were able to evaluate the process, through on-line monitoring of physical indexes involved or generated by the process, allowing intervention reduction and operator functions substitution.

SIMAP group objective is to carry through researches in intelligent manufacturing systems area, with the intention to promote the development of automatized devices in monitorization, diagnostic, decision taking and fabrication processes control which need a rigorous supervision with minimal human participation. Such activities aim the project, the conception, the implementation, adjustment and qualification of the monitoring systems qualification and machining processes automatization.

To achieve better performance, research lines were divided into 2 subgroups:

Machining processes monitoring: Obtain basic knowledge about the related aspects to the physical indexes generated on the machining processes, with the intention to develop and implement systems which aim the indirect on-line monitoring of such parameters.

Artificial intelligence techniques application on taking decisions: the information collected by multiple sensors, combined through artificial intelligence techniques, may aid the on-line diagnostic of a determined problem monitorized in a machining process, and later decision accords with the wished result.

DEVELOPMENT AREAS

  • CUTTING TOOLS DAMAGE DIAGNOSTIC BY MULTISENSORS
  • SENSORS FUSION THROUGH NEURAL NETS
  • DRILLING TOOL CONDITION MONITORIZATION IN DRILLING PROCESS
