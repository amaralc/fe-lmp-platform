Ultra precision Machining - USIPREC

DESCRIPTION

In 1986, Precision Engineering Laboratory started researches on ultra precision machining area within the project and construction of a machine-tool to produce photocopy cylinders. This machine, endowed with aerostatic bearings and guides and employing monocrystalline diamond tools, allowed the attainment of surfaces roughness Ra around 10nm.

The following years were then dedicated to improve the knowledge acquired in the ultra precision area, through the study of machining technology bases with diamond tools and the development of machines and elements of machines for the ultra precision machining, as aerostatic bearings, positioning systems and linear guides, among others. It also had been studied problems related to contact lens machining with polymer materials, employing a specific machine for this use, and adapted to machine in two axles of a Moore lathe initially projected to produce computer hard disks. Also the conception and fabrication of devices and benches for general precision mechanics, as well as study of different precision machining processes (single cutting edge reaming and precision boring, among others) were the research topic.

In 1993 a doctor work was initiated focusing ductile machining materials with hard and fragile behavior, mainly infrared crystals. On this work, conclude in 1997, a deep analysis was carried through of the different machining parameters influence and work variables over the superficial and optic quality, as well as over the machining strength components. In virtue of the lowermost signal of force verified in ultra precision machining processes, there was the need to develop an ultra small strength values measure system based in the piezoelectric principle.
